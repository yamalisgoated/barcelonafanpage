Camp Nou - Home of FC Barcelona

• Camp Nou is the iconic home of FC Barcelona and holds a rich history dating back to the 1950s.

• Construction of the stadium began on March 28, 1954, to accommodate the club’s growing fanbase.

• It was designed by architects Francesc Mitjans and Josep Soteras.

• Officially opened on September 24, 1957, with a seating capacity of approximately 93,000.

• In the 1970s and 80s, it was expanded and hosted historic events like the 1982 FIFA World Cup and the 1992 Olympics.

• The 1990s saw modernizations such as media boxes, VIP areas, and improved lighting.

• The “Dream Team” era under Johan Cruyff made Camp Nou a football fortress.

• In 2014, FC Barcelona approved the Espai Barça project to renovate the stadium completely.

• In 2023, renovation work began, and the team temporarily moved to Estadi Olímpic Lluís Companys.

• The new Camp Nou, expected in 2026, will feature a retractable roof, smart technology, and over 105,000 seats.

• It remains the largest stadium in Europe and a symbol of Catalan pride and football greatness.
